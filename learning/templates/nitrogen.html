Nitrogen

Importance of Nitrogen on Plant Nutrition

Nitrogen is a nutrient that promotes production of proteins, the main composition of tissues of most living organisms. Without proteins, plants wither and die [1].

In addition, nitrogen is a major component of chlorophyll, a pigment which colors leaves green. The chlorophyll is uniquely capable of converting energy from the sun to transform water, carbon dioxide and mineral as the plant’s only source of food [2].

Though nitrogen is abundant to earth’s atmosphere, is not readily available to be used as nutrient for plants since it follows a production cycle [3].

Deficiency in nitrogen can cause slow plant growth and poor vegetative frame which is essential for high crop yields.

Nitrogen Deficiency Symptoms

Nitrogen (N) deficiency symptoms include yellow discoloration of leaves from tip backward older leaves brown. The lower leaves may die prematurely while the top of the plan remains green[4].
